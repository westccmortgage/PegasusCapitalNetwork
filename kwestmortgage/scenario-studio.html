      Rate Buydown Strategy

      A temporary buydown usually lowers the payment for an initial period while the note rate remains the actual loan rate. The subsidy is typically funded upfront by an approved source, subject to program guidelines.

      Permanent Buydown

      • Lower rate for the life of the loan
      • Higher upfront cost
      • Break-even depends on how long you keep the loan

      Temporary Buydown

      • Lower payment for the first 1–2 years
      • Note rate does not permanently change
      • Often depends on seller / builder / lender credit availability

      Rates, points, credits, and buydown structures are assumptions for educational scenario planning only. They are not rate quotes, APRs, locked rates, approvals, or commitments to lend. Final terms depend on borrower qualification, property eligibility, market conditions, lender pricing, seller credits, program rules, underwriting approval, and current guidelines.
